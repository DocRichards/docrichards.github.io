# Teaching

I teach across the Harold Walter Siebens School of Business at Buena Vista University, with a focus on operations, leadership, agribusiness management, and introductory business.

## Current Courses

**BUSN 100: Contemporary Business and Free Enterprise**
An introductory course covering today's business environment, the free enterprise system, teamwork, decision-making, and the foundations of the Harold Walter Siebens School of Business.

**MGMT 220: Principles of Management**
Covers the core management functions: planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling, along with decision-making, communication, ethics, and international management.

**MGMT 465: Production and Operations Management**
Introduces the concepts and language of operations management, including inventory management, productivity, process analysis, supply chain, and logistics, with an emphasis on building competitive advantage.

**MGMT 485: Business Leadership**
A course I developed covering core leadership values, leadership styles, and practical application through experiential learning, group projects, and simulations.

**AGBU 225: Agriculture/Farm Business Management**
Covers agribusiness operations, planning, financial management, and case analysis applied to real-world problems in managing land, livestock, labor, and capital.

**IDST 400: Challenges for the 21st Century**
A culminating interdisciplinary seminar addressing emerging technologies, new paradigms for learning, and the challenges of a globalized world.

## Teaching Philosophy

I believe relationships and trust are prerequisites for learning. My classroom approach centers on facilitated discussion, with Socratic questioning used to sharpen thinking and push students past surface-level answers. I am committed to the developmental growth of every student I work with.
